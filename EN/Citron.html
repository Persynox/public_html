This lemon is not yellow!

As surprising as it may seem, the screen on which you are reading this article: computer, smartphone, tablet, whatever, does not produce any yellow light! Everything is an illusion!

The "real" yellow

A yellow object around us absorbs all the colors of light, except yellow, which it reflects. This yellow light is reflected back to our eye which makes us see yellow.

In order to capture these colors, the back of our eye is lined with light-sensitive receptors: the cones.

No, not these... but rather these:

These are of three types, each sensitive to a particular color:

  • S cones are sensitive to blue,

  • the M cones to green,

  • the L cones to red.

Thus, the light reflected by a yellow object activates our L cones and our M cones, so the brain reconstitutes yellow.
