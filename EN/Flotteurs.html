Have you ever seen these "floats"?

Have you ever seen these strange floating shapes? Have you ever wondered what it is? Here are some simple explanations!

Floating bodies

These strange shapes look like tiny transparent worms, and disappear if you try to look at them closely! This common phenomenon is called floating bodies.

No need to rinse your eyes, they exist inside your eyeball! These shapes seem to be alive since they move and change shape, but they are (fortunately) not!

Is it serious, doctor?

First of all, don't panic, these "floaters" are completely normal and benign. These are small objects that cast shadows on the retina (the sensitive tissue at the back of your eye).

They may be bits of tissue, red blood cells, or clumps of protein suspended in the vitreous humor (the gelatinous fluid that fills your eye).

If they move when we try to look at them, it is because they drift with the movements of the eye.

They are most easily noticed when looking at a uniformly clear surface: like a clear sky. Indeed, when we look at the sky, a large quantity of light enters our eye which causes the contraction of our pupil. Thus, the floats become sharper, just as when a diffuse light source is replaced by a concentrated light source: the projected shadow is sharper!

These floats remind us that what we see depends as much on our biology as on the outside world.
